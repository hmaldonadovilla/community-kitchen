This procedure must be read by all employees of the Community Kitchen.
It must be made available to volunteers involved in cleaning activities.

## RESPONSIBILITY
Cooks are responsible. Volunteers may help under supervision. Third party service providers may participate in some specific activities.
Kitchen Manager provides daily oversight. Operations Manager holds overall accountability.

>## IMPORTANT
>
>- Daily, weekly and monthly cleaning activities must be recorded in the Storage and Cleaning Checks app.
>- Daily cleaning is done after each shift, AM and PM.
>- Weekly and monthly cleaning follow the Cleaning Schedule. Any change to the person responsible for weekly or monthly cleaning must be requested and approved by the Kitchen Manager or Operations Manager.
>- If a cleaning activity could not be completed, inform the next shift and the Kitchen manager or Operations >Manager.
>- Immediately inform Kitchen Manager or Operations Manager when cleaning products, bin bags, tissues are >running low and/or cleaning cloth or equipment needs to be repaired/replaced.
>- Any issue → record in the app + inform Kitchen Manager or Operations Manager immediately.

## General Rules

### DO

- Wash hands before & after cleaning.
- Use only food-safe detergent / degreaser / disinfectant products.
- Use clean water.
- Respect product dosage and contact time
- Clean food areas first, floors & bins last.
- Clean → disinfect → rinse (if required).
- Clean spills immediately.
- Leave surfaces clean & dry.
- Wash dirty cloths, aprons and hats regularly.

### DO NOT

- Do not clean floors or bins before worktops.
- Do not use non food-safe, personal, home-made or industrial products.
- Do not reuse the same cloth for tables and floors.
- Do not use dirty cloths, aprons or hats.
- Do not leave cleaning products on worktops, portioning tables, near food/stove/utensils/fresh vegetables/dry goods.

## Daily AM & PM Shift

### DO

- Wash all used utensils & containers (pots, pans, lids, boards, dish boxes, buckets, oven trays, rice cooker bowls).
- Clean rice cooker exterior if splashed.
- Cool leftovers → seal → label → fridge/freezer.
- Remove expired or moldy products.
- Check for signs of pests (insects, rodents).
- Clean & disinfect tables, sinks, stove, splashbacks.
- Empty & clean dishwasher.
- Sweep & mop floors.
- Keep brooms clean.
- Empty and clean mop buckets.
- Keep cleaning room tidy.
- Discard damage equipment.
- Empty and clean bins.
- Bin lines replaced.
- Keep work clothes in designated closet.
- Wash dirty cloths, aprons and hats used during shift.
- Keep cleaning products in cleaning cupboard.

### DO NOT

- Do not assume previous shift cleaned enough.
- Do not leave dirty equipment and cloths overnight.
- Do not leave leftovers in the oven or anywhere in the kitchen.
- Do not skip cleaning activities or pest checks at end of shift.
- Do not leave bin without new bin bags.

## Weekly

### DO

- Deep clean plastic boards.
- Clean fridge/freezer shelves & handles.
- Clean cupboards, doors, switches.
- Deep clean ovens.
- Clean exhaust hood filters and accessible surfaces.
- Check pest monitoring points or traps if present.

### DO NOT

- Do not ignore damaged boards.
- Do not ignore dirt or grease buildup.
- Do not ignore pest signs or traps.

## Monthly

### DO

- Defrost & deep clean freezers.
- Clean pantry shelves.
- Discard damaged boards.
- Descale dishwasher.
- Deep clean exhaust hood (including hard-to-reach areas).

### DO NOT

- Do not wait for heavy ice buildup.
- Do not allow grease buildup in exhaust hood.
